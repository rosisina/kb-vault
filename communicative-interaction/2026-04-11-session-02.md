### 2.8 raw/07 OCR skip вҖ” Bates-numbering mapping approach (from James)

James proposed skipping full OCR of raw/07. Scanned files (13,495 pages, 4GB+) in favor of a page-number mapping approach.

Technical basis (provided by James):
- Each scanned page has a Record No. stamped in the upper right
- Stamps are generated via PDF functionality (not part of the underlying scan image)
- Numbers are **text-searchable within the PDF** вҖ” standard Bates numbering pattern used in legal discovery
- Angular frontend displays the actual scanned image on demand when users want to verify a citation

**Decision: skip OCR for raw/07.**

Reasoning:
1. **Evidence value is in the image itself.** Courts and readers trust the original scanned image, not OCR-reproduced text. OCR introduces errors and provides a reframing surface for challenges.
2. **Bates numbering is the industry-standard pattern.** Legal discovery globally uses this method. No OCR is needed to establish the citation chain вҖ” just the mapping.
3. **The mapping already partially exists.** `../defense-kg-platform/kg/evidence_record_mapping.json` already maps layers вҶ’ files + record ranges. Fine-grained mapping emerges naturally from PDF text search.
4. **Display is delegated to Phase B / Angular UI.** Wiki provides citation text (`Record No. 10,347`), Aurora KG stores `:Evidence {docPage: 10347, file: ...}`, Angular UI opens the PDF at that page. No text extraction required.
5. **4GB+ OCR cost is disproportionate to benefit.** Even with Google Document AI, full OCR of 13,495 Korean legal pages is expensive and produces low-value text (the image is still the legal evidence).

**Exception: on-demand tier-2 OCR.** For rare cases where a specific page's exact wording matters (e.g., a specific н”јмқҳмһҗмӢ л¬ёмЎ°м„ң passage cited by the MPO, or a specific KIDA м—°кө¬ліҙкі м„ң sentence that needs textual diff against US DoD OT&E regulation), Aurora's `02b_ocr_document_ai.py` (Google Document AI) can be invoked for that specific page only. This is a tiered approach: tier-0 (mapping, 99% of cases) + tier-1 (PDF text search) + tier-2 (Google Document AI on demand).

Aurora OCR discovery (per James's question): `defense-kg-platform/pipeline/` contains `02b_ocr_document_ai.py` (Google Document AI processor `f9a24f4c113d6e34`), `02b_ocr_reprocess.py`, `02c_ocr_validate.py`. Aurora already has production-grade OCR infrastructure set up. It was (correctly) not used for full 07 processing вҖ” same judgment James reached now.
