# Fraud Investigation (사기수사)

**Source:** `raw/01. book-beyond-cybersecurity/vault-converted-english/16-3-6-36-Sixth-Layer.md` • `raw/01. book-beyond-cybersecurity/vault-legacy-wiki-english/Fraud-Investigation.md` (legacy synthesis)

**Fraud Investigation (사기수사)** is a novel legal concept introduced in *Beyond Cybersecurity*. It refers to **organized deceptive investigation** conducted by state power agencies through abuse of investigative authority — not an investigation into fraud, but an investigation that is itself fraudulent. The concept is the legal core of Layer 6: the Military Prosecutor's Office (군 검찰단) investigation of the author is argued to satisfy the statutory elements of criminal fraud.

## Definition

Per the book's formal definition at `raw/01. book-beyond-cybersecurity/vault-converted-korean/12-3-6-36-제6층위-군.md` line 26:

> **Fraud Investigation (사기수사)** — an act in which state power agencies abuse their investigative authority **to promote organizational interest (조직의 이익을 도모하고)** and to conduct organized and deceptive investigations, thereby stigmatizing a specific individual as a criminal and inflicting **irrecoverable harm (회복 불가능한 손해)** on the victim.

The book's definition has **four constitutive elements**, all of which must be present for the concept to apply: (1) abuse of investigative authority, (2) the purpose is to promote organizational interest, (3) the investigation is organized and deceptive, and (4) the harm inflicted is irrecoverable. Earlier wiki drafts omitted elements (2) and (4); patched on 2026-04-11 A.6 Re-verify to restore the full definitional structure per book line 26.

The concept bridges two legal domains: **criminal fraud** (a private-law concept about deception for property gain) and **investigative authority abuse** (a public-law concept about misuse of state power). The book argues that in the distributed-agency configuration of the Defense Informatization Cartel, these two domains converge.

## Criminal element analysis

The book argues the MPO's investigation satisfies both the elements of criminal fraud and the characteristics of cybercrime.

### Criminal fraud elements applied to the investigation

- **Deceptive act** — investigation built on the false premise that four distinct KIATIS environments were identical (see [[kiatis-systems#the-four-environment-comparison-layer-6-core-evidence|four-environment comparison]]).
- **Inducing error** — creating the appearance that a fair investigation was underway when the premise was known to be false.
- **Property damage (법익 침해)** — dishonorable discharge, social death, and the destruction of the author's professional standing.

### Cybercrime characteristics applied to the institutional attack

- **Anonymity** — individual actors hidden behind organizational structures; no single prosecutor personally signs the entire chain of decisions.
- **Non-contact nature** — indirect attacks through documents and procedures, not face-to-face accusation.
- **Breadth** — simultaneous manipulation across multiple agencies (see [[defense-informatization-cartel|Defense Informatization Cartel]]).
- **Speed** — false report → administrative isolation → formal investigation chain executed rapidly after the 2022-02-08 meeting that exposed the old KIATIS Internet exposure. *(Note: the specific "within 48 hours" window is a Layer 5 temporal-forensics claim anchored to 기록 제11,334 / 제3,965 under the proposed atom `layer5-48hr-retaliation-causal-link.md`; the Layer 6 chapter treats the retaliation timing at a higher level of abstraction. This hub's 48-hour framing previously asserted it at Layer 6 — corrected on 2026-04-11 A.6 Re-verify to route the specific window to the Layer 5 atom.)*

## DIDC Center 1 vs Center 2

A key factual claim: the actual origin of the 2016 DIDC hacking was **DIDC Center 1 (Yongin)**, where old KIATIS was hosted as a parasite within the Defense Internet Homepage server. The public announcement named DIDC **Center 2 (Gyeryongdae)**. The book alleges that the prosecution knew this and concealed it, because tracing the hacking to Center 1 would expose the old KIATIS's 15-year Internet exposure (see [[kiatis-systems|KIATIS Systems]]).

## Regulations allegedly violated

- Fair investigation obligations under the Military Court Act (군사법원법)
- Military investigation human-rights protection regulations
- Due process principles
- Warrant requirements

## Routine Activities Theory applied to state crime

The book applies criminological Routine Activities Theory (motivated offender + suitable target + absence of capable guardianship = crime) to state crime:

- **Motivated offender** — cartel members with concealment motive (preventing accountability for 15 years of cybersecurity violations).
- **Suitable target** — the author, who held direct technical knowledge of the old KIATIS system reality.
- **Absence of guardianship** — the institutional failure of oversight bodies (Minister of National Defense, Military Prosecutor General, National Human Rights Commission, Anti-Corruption and Civil Rights Commission) documented in Layer 7.

This theoretical application is part of the book's claim that fraud investigation is not a malfunction of state power but a **structurally predictable outcome** under specific organizational conditions.
