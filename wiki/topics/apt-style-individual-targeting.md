# APT-Style Individual Targeting

**APT-Style Individual Targeting** is a theoretical concept introduced in the book *Beyond Cybersecurity* that extends the cybersecurity notion of Advanced Persistent Threats (APT) into the organizational, legal, and socio-psychological domains. The book's central thesis is that the tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) of cyber APTs can be **replicated in the physical world against a single individual** through coordinated institutional action.

## The four defining APT characteristics applied to individual targeting

| Cyber APT characteristic | Manifestation in organizational individual targeting |
|---|---|
| **Targeted** | Many-to-one attack structure focused on a single individual |
| **Persistent** | Long-term, phased attack spanning ~4 years (2019–2023) |
| **Stealthy** | Concealed behind legitimate administrative procedures |
| **Multi-stage** | Systematic attack phases across 7 distinct layers |

## Operating mechanisms

1. **Division of roles** — each agency within the cartel performs a specific function, so no single actor visibly commits the whole offense (see [[defense-informatization-cartel|Defense Informatization Cartel]]).
2. **Diffusion of responsibility** — the multi-agency structure reduces the moral burden felt by any individual actor.
3. **Cognitive dissonance management** — self-justification mechanisms for harmful actions within the cartel.
4. **Moral disengagement** — dehumanization of the targeted individual to bypass conscience.

## The cyber-physical parallel

The book begins from the theoretical premise that cyberspace mirrors human behavioral patterns. The cyber attack chain — **reconnaissance → infiltration → persistence → exfiltration** — is replicated in the physical world:

- **Reconnaissance** — identifying the target's position, duties, and vulnerabilities within the organization.
- **Infiltration** — setting up a scapegoat structure through position changes and duty transfers (Layer 3 per the [[7-layer-proof-system|7-Layer Proof System]]).
- **Persistence** — long-term attack via false power-abuse reports, administrative isolation, and manipulated investigation (Layers 5, 6).
- **Exfiltration** — the physical-world analog of data exfiltration is **social death**: dishonorable discharge and criminal stigmatization (Layer 6, 7).

## Key Takeaways

- [진리성] The book proposes that APT-style attack geometry — targeted, persistent, stealthy, multi-stage — is not unique to cyberspace; it can be executed by coordinated institutional actors against a single individual.
- [진리성] The attack phases observed in the book's case (reconnaissance 2019 → infiltration early 2020 → persistence 2020–2022 → exfiltration via deferred prosecution 2023) map one-to-one to the classical cyber attack chain.
- [진실성] The "division of roles / diffusion of responsibility" mechanism is what distinguishes APT-style individual targeting from a conventional conspiracy: no single actor sees the whole, which is why attribution and prosecution fail at the level of individual intent.
- [타당성] The framework has implications for whistleblower protection law: current legal protections assume a single offender, not a distributed institutional offense; the book's reform proposals (see [[whistleblower-protection-and-reform|Whistleblower Protection and Reform]]) address this gap.
- [진리성] This framework is novel in Korean academic literature and represents one of the book's principal theoretical contributions (alongside [[banality-vs-sophistication-of-evil|Sophistication of Evil]] and [[fraud-investigation|Fraud Investigation]]).
