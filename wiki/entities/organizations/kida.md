**KIDA (Korea Institute for Defense Analyses, 한국국방연구원)** is South Korea's principal defense policy research institute. It is identified in the 7-layer proof system as a **central Layer 4 actor** for producing a research report that allegedly fabricated academic justification for directive-level changes to the DT&E/OT&E separation regime. Per James's contextual input (2026-04-11) — **corrected framing** after A.5 analysis: KIDA cited the US document **DOD Operational Test and Evaluation (OT&E) for Information and Business Systems (Gilmore memo, 2010-09-14)** as authority for merging DT&E and OT&E into a single test activity. The US memo is actually an **OT&E-side authority document** (authored by DOT&E, the operational test executive) that permits OT&E to **reuse DT&E data** within a structured risk framework — NOT permission to erase the DT&E/OT&E boundary. The Korean 제2129호 제58조 ¶2 separation principle already implements the US framework faithfully; KIDA's citation is therefore best characterized as **category confusion** (OT&E data-reuse permission cited as if it justified DT&E/OT&E merger), not selective omission. If confirmed on source ingest of the KIDA report itself, this creates a textually verifiable category error in KIDA's representation of the US standard.
